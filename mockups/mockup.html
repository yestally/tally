Q3 Competitive Analysis — Notion, Coda, Mem.ai

A landscape view of how each competitor is positioning workspace + AI features as of Q3, and what gaps we should claim in our Q4 narrative.

1. Executive summary

P

Across the three competitors, the dominant Q3 narrative has shifted from "AI as a feature" to "AI as the surface". Notion is leading on integration breadth (+47% AI-touched documents QoQ), while Mem.ai has doubled down on a single-pane chat metaphor that is showing weak retention beyond week 2.

S

The data tells a sharper story: every competitor is converging on the same UX (a sidebar of agents + a canvas), but none have solved the oversight problem. This is the gap to claim — not "more AI", but "AI you can audit and steer".

2. Findings by competitor

  • Notion — Q3 launch of "AI Connectors" drove a 23% lift in WAU, but support tickets about hallucinated answers grew 3.1×.
  • Coda — Repositioning to "the doc that runs your workflows". Pricing now bundles agent runs by seat; we project margin pressure on lower tiers.
  • Mem.ai — Pivoted hard to a chat-first interface. Day-30 retention dropped from 38% to 21% per third-party panels.
